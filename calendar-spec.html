Family Calendar & Planner — Redesign Spec

Design specification for the Plan tab — informed by research into Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, Fantastical, Cozi, and TimeTree.

Overview & Design Philosophy

The current planner has good bones — month grid, event chips, life areas, seasonal nudges — but it feels squeezed and isn't optimised for the shared family use case. The redesign has three goals:

📅

Feel like a real calendar

The month grid should be spacious, clean, and instantly readable — not a small widget buried in a page. Event density should be communicated visually, not with truncated text.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Built for households, not individuals

Every event belongs to someone. Colour coding by family member — not by category — is the primary visual language. At a glance you should see whose week is busy.

⚡

Fast to add, easy to scan

Adding an event should be 3 taps. Scanning the week should take 2 seconds. Everything else — life areas, nudges, cost estimates — is secondary and collapsible.

💡
Core principle: The calendar grid is for navigation and density. Event details live in the agenda list below. Never cram full text into grid cells.

✓ Option A — Recommended

Month + Agenda

Compact month grid at the top gives household-level overview. Agenda list below shows what's coming up with full event details. Tapping any day scrolls the agenda to that date.

Best for: Planning-oriented users, seeing the full month at a glance, identifying busy periods.

⚡ Option B — Simpler

Week Strip + Agenda

Horizontal day strip replaces the month grid. Less context, but faster to navigate week by week. Good for users who check schedules daily rather than plan monthly.

Best for: Quick daily check-ins, less planning-oriented households.

Month Grid — Detailed Spec

1

Grid cells — no text, dots only

The most important decision in calendar UX

Each cell shows the date number and up to 3 coloured dots representing the first 3 distinct family members with events that day. If more than 3 members or events exist, a "+N" text indicator appears. No event titles in the grid — ever.

✓ Do this

  • 3 coloured dots max per cell
  • One dot per unique member colour
  • "+2" overflow indicator
  • Today = filled circle on date number
  • Selected day = light tint background

✗ Not this

  • Event title text in cells
  • Category emoji chips
  • Expanding cells to show more
  • Per-event dots (use per-member)
  • Coloured full-width bars (too heavy)
⚠️
Dots represent members, not events. If Chris has 3 events today, that's still 1 blue dot. This keeps the grid readable at all densities.
2

Grid height — fixed, not expanding

Each cell has a fixed min-height of 64px on mobile. The grid always shows 5–6 rows (the full month). It does not expand or collapse rows. Consistent height = predictable layout, no jarring reflows.

The month grid should take up approximately 40% of the screen on a standard iPhone. The remaining 60% is the agenda list.

3

Tapping a day

Tapping a day in the month grid does two things simultaneously:

  • Highlights the cell with a light background tint
  • Scrolls the agenda list below to that date — no page transition, no modal, no separate "day view"
✓
This is the Google Calendar pattern. It keeps both views visible simultaneously — the month for orientation, the agenda for detail.

Week View

The week view should be a 7-day horizontal strip that appears between the header and the agenda when the user switches to Week mode. It replaces the month grid, not the agenda.

1

Day strip design

Each day column shows: abbreviated day name, date number, and coloured dots. Scrollable horizontally so users can swipe back/forward through weeks. Today is always visible in the initial position.

ElementSpec
Day name3-letter, uppercase, 10px, muted
Date number18px, bold, dark
Today indicatorFilled accent circle behind date number
Selected indicatorAccent border or background tint
Event dotsSame as month grid — per-member, max 3
Cell width44px minimum (iOS tap target)
💡
View toggle: A small "Month / Week" pill toggle in the header switches between the two calendar types. The agenda list always remains visible below regardless of which view is active.

Supporting Sections — Life Areas & Nudges

These sections are valuable but should not dominate the screen. Both are collapsible and sit below the calendar, not above it.

1

Life Areas — compact horizontal chips

Was: large tile grid. Now: scrollable chip strip

Instead of a grid of large tiles, Life Areas appear as a horizontally-scrollable row of small chips. Each chip shows the category emoji, name, and a count of upcoming events. Tapping jumps to that category's next event in the agenda.

2

Heads up from Toto 🐕 — keep but slim

Seasonal nudge cards remain — same horizontal scroll, same size

The nudge card strip is a great concept — keep it. The cards are already compact (200px wide). The section just needs to be moved below the calendar and Life Areas, not between them. It's a nice-to-have discovery feature, not primary navigation.

⚠️
Nudge cards should only appear when there's something relevant in the next 45 days. Don't show the section header with an empty card list.
