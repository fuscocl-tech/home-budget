Family Calendar

Design specification — a shared calendar built for family life, optimised for side-by-side day view on iPad and ambient display on TV. Engagement, experience, and ease of use are the design north stars.

🎯
Design Principles
The four rules every decision is measured against
Foundation▼
1 — Glanceable
You should understand today in under 3 seconds. No scanning, no hunting. The right information is immediately obvious.
2 — Personal
Every person in the household is a first-class citizen. Their colour, their column, their events — unmistakably theirs.
3 — Effortless to add
Adding an event should take under 10 seconds. Natural language, smart defaults, minimal tapping. Like Google Calendar at its best.
4 — Beautiful on every screen
Looks great on a phone, excellent on an iPad, and genuinely stunning in ambient TV mode. Not an afterthought on any device.
💡 Google Calendar as the benchmark: Google Calendar succeeds because it gets out of the way — adding events is frictionless, the visual hierarchy is clear, and it handles complex recurring events without making the user think. Toto's calendar must match that ease of use, then layer in the family-specific features that Google doesn't have: per-person columns, kids' events, shared routines, and TV ambient mode.

📺 Auto-activating TV mode: When Toto detects it's running on an Apple TV or a browser connected to a large display, it automatically enters ambient mode. On iPad, a "Display mode" button in the top corner locks the screen on and switches to the TV layout — useful when the iPad is on a kitchen stand.

Event Creation — Frictionless by Design
Natural language, smart defaults, under 10 seconds
UX priority▼

The single most important factor in whether people maintain a calendar is how easy it is to add events. If it takes more than 10 seconds, people default to their phone's native calendar. Toto's event creation must be the fastest in the category.

💡 Natural language parsing: "Mia swimming Thursday 3:30" should parse to Mia's column, Thursday, 3:30pm, with "Swimming" as the event name. "Dentist tomorrow 2pm Chris" → Chris, tomorrow, 2pm. "Family dinner Friday 7" → all members, Friday 7pm. Failures fall back gracefully to the standard form pre-filled with whatever was typed.

Three ways to add an event

1 — Quick add (text)
Type naturally. AI parses name, time, date, and person. Fastest path — ideal for phone.
2 — Tap a time slot
Tap in a person's column at a specific time. Pre-fills person and time. Just type the name and save. Ideal for iPad.
3 — Full form
"More options" expands to a complete event form for complex recurring events, travel time, attachments.

💡 Drag to reschedule is the single most-loved gesture in Google Calendar. Users should be able to long-press any event and drag it to a new time — the event snaps to 15-minute increments. Dragging across columns changes the assigned person. This makes the calendar feel truly live and editable, not just a display.

Integration with the Rest of Toto
How the calendar connects every other feature together
Architecture▼

The calendar isn't just another section — it's the connective tissue of Toto. Done right, it makes every other feature more useful by giving it time context.

☀️
Today screen: Pulls today's events from the calendar to populate the schedule section of the morning briefing. Without this connection, the Today screen can't show today's schedule.
🍝
Meal planner: "Dinner tonight" on the TV display comes from the meal plan. When a meal is planned, an optional calendar event is created — "Family dinner 6:30pm — Spag bol". This syncs the kitchen and the schedule.
🛒
Shopping list: When a meal event is on the calendar, the missing ingredients are surfaced in the shopping list on the day before. "Mince and pasta needed for tomorrow's dinner" appears on Wednesday's Today screen.
💰
Budget & bills: Bills with due dates create calendar events automatically. Pay day creates a calendar event. Upcoming project stage payments appear on the calendar. Financial events are colour-coded in Toto teal.
📋
Kids' tasks: A chore with a due date appears on the child's calendar column. "Make bed — daily" shows as a recurring all-day event in their column. Seeing it on the calendar alongside school and sport gives kids a better sense of ownership over their day.
⏰
Morning routines: Routine checklist items with times ("Lunch packed by 8:15am") appear on the family calendar for the relevant people — making routines a first-class calendar citizen, not a separate to-do list.
🚗
Vehicle reminders: Rego expiry and insurance renewal dates from the Home section are automatically added to the family calendar — no manual entry, no surprises.
🎉
Countdowns: A countdown event ("57 days to Bali") on the calendar shows the departure day as a family event with a 🌴 emoji — and it's the same data that feeds the countdown on the TV display and the Today screen.
ℹ️ External calendar sync: Google Calendar and Apple Calendar events import into Toto's calendar with a neutral grey colour, distinguishable from Toto-created events. Users can assign imported events to household members to give them a colour. Two-way sync (editing in Toto reflects in Google/Apple) is a later-phase feature — read-only import ships first.
