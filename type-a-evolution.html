Strategy · Type A Mode

How Type A evolves

Type A Mode has solid bones — a life score, daily missions, and a weekly ritual. But several recently built features aren't connected to it yet, the scoring model has gaps, and the experience stops short of what it could be. This document outlines a phased evolution — from quick fixes to a genuinely differentiated coaching product.

● Fix now — before next release

Things that are already broken

These aren't enhancements — the current scoring model references data structures that have changed. They should be fixed before the mode is used by real users.

🛒
Shopping list dimension uses the wrong data source
RECENTLY BUILT · Shopping Lists (Section 10)
The Family dimension currently checks state.meals.shopping[] (old system) and state.meals.pantry[] (also old) to evaluate household readiness. Both of these are now deprecated — all shopping data lives in state.lists.food.items[] with item states of active, got_it, and not_found. Pantry is no longer a separate array.
Current: checks state.meals.shopping.length > 0 — always 0 now
Fix: score based on state.lists.food.items — active count, got_it ratio, and whether the list is being used at all
A well-stocked, active list (≥5 items, some got_it) = 80+ score. Empty list = 20.
📋
Adult routines are not scored at all
RECENTLY BUILT · Routines on Today screen
Adult routines (My Routines on the Today screen) have a full completion and streak system — routine.completions[todayKey], _routineStreak(routine) — but none of this feeds into the Life Score. A user who completes their morning routine every day gets no Life Score credit for it.
Current: routines have zero weight in any dimension
Fix: add routine completion % to the Family or a new Habits dimension
Data available: _routineStreak(routine), _routineCompletedToday(routine)
● Near term — next 1–2 releases

Two new dimensions

The current 5-dimension model covers Budget, Meals, Home, Family, Goals. Two significant parts of the app — routines/habits and the calendar — contribute nothing to the score. These should become first-class dimensions.

⚡
New dimension: Habits
SOURCES: state.routines · routine.completions · _routineStreak()
Daily routine consistency is one of the most visible, high-frequency signals in the app. It's something users interact with every day on the Today screen — far more frequently than budgets or maintenance. Making it a scored dimension gives the score a daily heartbeat and makes Type A Mode feel live rather than weekly.
+40 pts — Today's routines completed (% of active routines fully checked off)
+40 pts — Streak health: 0–10 day streak, scaled. 10+ days = full 40pts.
+20 pts — Has at least one routine set up with steps
If no routines exist: 20pt default (not penalised, just not rewarded)
Mission: "Complete your morning routine" → direct action, high frequency, immediately rewarding
📅
New dimension: Plan
SOURCES: state.planner.events · bills · state.documents
The Planner has been significantly upgraded — agenda view, member filtering, address fields, category colours. But it contributes nothing to the Life Score. A "Plan" dimension would reward households that are actively using their calendar and are on top of upcoming commitments — not just reacting to what's due today.
+40 pts — Has events planned in the next 7 days
+30 pts — No bills overdue (all bills.days ≥ 0)
+30 pts — No documents expired
Mission: "Add something to your calendar this week" or "Renew [expired doc]"
This replaces the current situation where expired docs drag down the Home score — it's a more logical home for them
Revised 7-dimension model
💰 Budget
🍽 Meals
🏠 Home
👨‍👩‍👧 Family
🎯 Goals
⚡ Habits new
📅 Plan new
Total still = average of all dimensions. Adding 2 new dimensions slightly dilutes each existing one — good, because it means a bad Budget week doesn't tank the whole score.
● Near term

Score model improvements

The current scoring works but has some rough edges that would frustrate users in practice.

📸
Score history snapshots
The score is recalculated live but never saved. One weekly snapshot — stored at reset completion — would unlock a simple trend line. "8 weeks ago: 48% → today: 72%" is powerful motivation. Store as an array of { date, score } objects in state.settings.typeAHistory[].
Snapshot taken on Weekly Reset completion
Keep last 12 weeks (rolling window)
Simple sparkline on the Life Score card
🔍
Dimension drill-down
Right now the Life Score card shows a ring and a tagline. Tapping it should open a sheet showing all 7 dimensions — their individual scores, what's dragging each one down, and a direct action button. Users currently can't tell why their score is what it is.
Each dimension row shows its score, a mini bar, and the tip text
Tap a dimension row → navigate to that tab
Uses the same sheet pattern as Heads Up and Slipping
✅
Mission completion feedback loop
Completing a mission currently just resets the tracking state silently. The user gets no confirmation, no score delta, no celebration. This breaks the core reward loop — the whole point of a mission is that it moves the score. After completing a mission, show a brief toast or card: "Budget dimension: +30 pts · Life Score: 58% → 72%".
Current
User taps "Do it now" → navigates to Budget tab. No feedback. Score quietly changes on next render. User doesn't know it worked.
Proposed
User completes action → returns to Today → toast shows "Life Score went up 14 pts 🎉" with before/after. Score ring animates. Feels earned.
● Medium term

Smarter missions

The current mission engine is rule-based and static. These improvements make it feel more responsive and personal without needing full AI.

🕐
Time-aware missions
Missions are currently the same regardless of when you open the app. A morning mission should be different from an evening one. On a Sunday evening the right mission is "plan next week's meals" — not "log your actuals".
Morning: action-oriented — routines, logging, scheduling
Evening: review-oriented — check tomorrow's calendar, plan lunchboxes
Sunday: weekly focus — meal plan, reset, bills review
🎯
Mission streaks
Currently only the Weekly Reset earns a streak. Completing daily missions could earn their own streak — shown as a secondary badge on the Life Score card. "3-day mission streak" creates a daily pull that complements the weekly reset habit.
New key: typeAMissionStreak — increments on mission completion, resets if skipped 2 days running
Shown as ⚡ badge alongside the 🔥 weekly streak
🤖
AI-generated mission explanations
USES: existing Toto AI assistant (Claude)
The current mission sub-text is hardcoded ("Import a bank CSV or add manually"). The existing AI assistant already knows the user's household state. It could generate a one-sentence personalised explanation: "You haven't logged any spending this month — your budget score is stuck at 30% until you do." This makes the mission feel like advice, not instructions.
Runs as a background call after mission card renders — same pattern as _fetchAIBriefing()
Falls back to existing hardcoded sub-text if the call fails or takes too long
Could be gated behind a paid tier (Enhanced AI add-on)
● Medium term

Weekly Reset evolution

The 5-step reset wizard works, but it's static and doesn't reflect the household's actual state well enough. Two clear improvements.

📊
Add recently built sections to the reset
The reset was built before Shopping Lists and Planner were fully developed. It should now include:
Shopping list check — how many active items? Is the list up to date? Tap to open Lists.
Calendar review — does next week have events planned? Any conflicts? Tap to open Planner.
Kids check-in — any pending approvals? How are streaks tracking? Tap to open Kids.
Steps that are already "all clear" could auto-collapse so the reset only shows what needs attention
✍️
AI weekly summary
USES: Claude · existing AI assistant
At the start of the reset, the AI generates a 2–3 sentence summary of the past week. This transforms the reset from a form into a coach check-in. Example: "You had a strong week on budget — spending was 12% under. Meals were unplanned for 4 days. The kids completed 80% of their routines. This week: focus on meal planning first."
Pulls from Life Score dimension tips, bills, planner events, kids completions
Async — shows a loading skeleton while generating, doesn't block the reset flow
Natural candidate for the Enhanced AI paid add-on
◆ Future

Future features that belong in Type A

These are new product features that would be most powerful as part of Type A Mode — either unlocking new dimensions or creating new engagement loops.

💬
Health & Wellbeing dimension
If TOTO ever adds sleep, exercise, or mood tracking (even lightweight self-reported check-ins), it would be a natural 8th dimension. A daily "how are you feeling?" prompt on the Today screen — answered in one tap — gives the Life Score a personal layer beyond household logistics. Score based on consistency of check-ins + self-reported wellbeing trend.
🏆
Family challenges
Type A already has missions for the adult. A natural extension is shared household challenges — things the whole family participates in. "Complete every dinner this week as a family", "Hit 80% on the Life Score for 4 consecutive weeks". These would show in the Kids section as well, reinforcing the connection between kids' chores and the household score.
📈
Benchmarking & community
ALIGNS WITH: growth strategy — referrals, community engagement
Anonymous opt-in benchmarking: "Your household scores higher than 68% of families in the same life stage." This connects to the growth strategy's community angle and gives the Life Score external reference. Referral bonus: when you refer someone, if their score improves, you both get streak credit.
🔔
Proactive push notifications
Type A Mode users are the most engaged segment. They're the right audience for proactive nudges: "Your Life Score dropped 8 points this week — your Meals score is dragging. Tap to fix it." or "You're 2 days from breaking your weekly reset streak." These are high-value, low-noise notifications that justify the mode existing as a distinct tier.
◆ Monetisation

Type A as a paid product

Type A Mode is currently free with no gate. Given its depth and the engagement it drives, it's a strong candidate for a paid tier — but the rollout matters.

Type A Mode → "TOTO Pro"
Type A Mode isn't just a settings toggle — it's a fundamentally different product experience. A user with Type A enabled gets a life coach in their pocket. That's a value proposition worth paying for, and it's distinct enough from the free tier to justify a separate brand within the product.

The existing monetisation strategy already includes "Enhanced AI" and "Periodic 2-week full unlock" as paid mechanics. Type A Mode is the natural home for both of these — the 2-week unlock becomes a 2-week TOTO Pro trial, and Enhanced AI adds the personalised mission explanations and weekly summaries described above.

Recommended rollout
Now: Keep Type A free. Fix the broken scoring. Add the Habits and Plan dimensions. Make the mode feel polished and trustworthy first.
Next: Add score history, dimension drill-down, and mission feedback. These are the features that make the score feel real. Ship a 2-week Pro trial.
Later: Add AI summaries and push nudges. Gate these behind the paid tier. The free features are still compelling; the paid layer adds genuine intelligence.
Don't gate the Life Score itself — it's the hook. Gate the intelligence layer around it (AI explanations, trend history, nudges). A user who can see their score but can't see why it's low is highly motivated to upgrade.
