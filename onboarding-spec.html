Onboarding Experience

Design specification — how a new Toto user goes from first login to a fully configured household.

Section Naming — Beyond "Wallet"

The current "Wallet" section covers Budget, Bills, Net Worth, Goals, Insights, and more — far broader than a wallet. Here are additional options beyond the first round:

Onboarding Principles

Before diving into the flow, these are the design principles that should guide every decision:

⚡

Speed to value

The user should see a personalised dashboard within 3 minutes. Defer everything non-essential.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

People first

Ask who's in the household early — it shapes every other feature (kids chores, lunchbox, meals).

🎯

Minimum required

Only ask what Toto absolutely needs to function. Everything else can be added later from within the app.

🪄

Smart defaults

Pre-fill amounts based on home situation (renting vs mortgage etc.) so users edit rather than type from scratch.

🔁

Always escapable

Every step has a Skip or Back option. Never trap the user — they can complete setup from Settings later.

✨

Celebrate completion

Finish with a moment of delight — a personalised summary screen that makes the household feel real.

Post-Onboarding: Next Steps

Some things are genuinely better collected after the user has used the app. These should be surfaced as friendly prompts within the relevant section — not bolted onto onboarding.

?

Net Worth Setup

Assets (savings, super, property value) and liabilities (mortgage balance, car loans)

Post-onboarding

Net Worth requires detailed financial information the user may not have to hand. Show a friendly prompt banner in the Net Worth tab: "Add your assets and liabilities to see your full financial picture →" rather than asking during setup.

?

Vehicles & Documents

Rego dates, insurance renewals, important document expiry tracking

Post-onboarding

These are high-value features but too detailed for first-run. A prompt in the Home section is sufficient: "Add your vehicles and documents to get renewal reminders."

?

Savings Goals

Holiday fund, emergency buffer, renovation savings, etc.

Post-onboarding

Goals are meaningful once the user understands their surplus. Prompt from the Goals tab after 7 days or once a surplus is established: "You have $X surplus — want to set a savings goal?"

?

Chores & Prizes

Assigning chores to kids, setting up the rewards store

Post-onboarding

Kid profiles are created in onboarding — that's enough. Assigning specific chores and prizes is too granular for first-run. The Kids tab should open with a guided empty state that walks parents through their first chore setup.

💡 Setup completeness indicator: A "Setup Progress" card on the dashboard combined with contextual to-do prompts inside each section is the ideal combination — discoverable without being pushy. See mock-ups below.

Household Access & Multi-User

When a second adult or older teenager is added to the household, they should be able to access the same shared data — but the way they log in and what they see should differ by role.

Adult access — invite by email

Adults get a standard account linked to their email. The household owner sends an invite; the invitee clicks a link, sets a password, and lands on the same shared household data. Both adults see everything by default, but either can mark individual income sources as private (e.g. hide a salary from the other adult).

Child access — PIN + avatar login (under 16)

Children don't need an email account. Instead, a parent sets up a child profile with a 4-digit PIN during onboarding or from the Kids settings. On the login screen, the child taps their avatar and enters their PIN — no typing an email, no password manager, no complexity.

🔒 Security model: Child PINs are stored as server-side hashed values tied to the household ID, not as standalone accounts. A child cannot access the household without a parent first logging in on the device and "handing it over" — or by selecting the child profile on the household's shared login screen. PINs should be rate-limited (3 attempts, then 30-second lockout).

Child Onboarding — First Login Experience

A child's profile is created by a parent during household onboarding or from the Kids settings. Their "onboarding" is the moment they first tap their avatar on the login screen and set up their PIN. It must be simple enough for a 6-year-old, fun enough that they want to do it, and secure enough that a parent trusts it.

🔒 Parent gate: Before a child sees the PIN setup screen for the first time, the app requires a parent to authenticate (enter their own PIN or biometric). This prevents a child from setting up their own profile without parental approval. The parent gate only appears on first setup — not on every subsequent login.
