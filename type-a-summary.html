NEW FEATURE

toto Type A Mode

Turns Toto from a passive tracker into an active personal coach. It tells you what to do, when to do it, and gets in your face if you ignore it.

What it does

Three components that work together

1

Life Score

A percentage showing how "on top of things" you are — calculated across everything in Toto. It's gamified. You want to get to 100%.

Budget
Income set up, expenses tracked, actuals logged
Meals
This week planned, shopping list generated
Home
Maintenance current, rego up to date, documents tracked
Family
School lunches planned, pantry stocked
Goals
Savings goals set and progressing

Each dimension shows a progress bar, a specific tip on what's dragging it down, and a "Fix" link that takes you straight there. The ring gauge gives you the overall number.

2

Daily Mission

One specific task per day, picked automatically based on your weakest Life Score area. Things like:

  • "Plan this week's dinners — takes 2 minutes"
  • "Log actual spending this month"
  • "Mark pest control as done"
  • "Set your first savings goal"

The escalation is the key part:

Day 1

Card on the Today screen. "Let's do it" or "Not today".

Day 2+

Full-screen lightbox pops up. "This has been waiting 2 days." Can't miss it.

You either do it or actively dismiss it. The app doesn't let you pretend it's not there.

3

Weekly Reset

A 5-minute guided wizard that walks you through getting your week sorted:

Review this month's spending
Shows actual vs budgeted with live numbers
Plan next week's meals
Shows how many meals are planned, links to planner
Check the pantry
Shows items needing restock, links to stocktake
Review upcoming bills
Shows bills due this week with total amount
Household maintenance
Shows overdue items, links to maintenance tracker

Each step shows live data and links to the relevant feature. Skip what you don't need. Completing it builds a streak counter — motivates consistency week to week.

The idea behind it

Why this matters

Most people want to be organised but don't know what to do next. Type A mode removes the thinking — Toto decides what's most important and walks you through it, one thing at a time.

The gap isn't tools. Most people have a budget app, a calendar, a notes app, a shopping list. The gap is knowing the next action. They open the app, see data, think "yeah I should do something", then close it.

Type A mode shifts Toto from "here's your data" to "here's what to do right now." The daily mission gives you one thing. The weekly reset gives you five things. The life score makes it a game. The escalation makes sure you don't ignore it.

It's the difference between a dashboard and a coach.

When it's off — none of this appears. The app works exactly the same as before. It's opt-in, not forced. Toggle it on in Settings when you're ready.
