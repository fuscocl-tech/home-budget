Wagepay — what we're stealing

Confidence-by-numbers. Big white hero figures on a deep card. Circular dial for scores. Mint pill CTAs.

Betashares Direct — what we're stealing

Horizontal stacked allocation bar (not a pie). Bold ALL-CAPS ticker codes. Bento grid with thin coloured top stripes. Generous corner radius.

Screen 01

Today — the morning briefing

The dark hero card answers the one question Chris actually has at 7am: how much can I spend this week without breaking anything? Everything else on the screen is a card answering "what do I need to know about today" in one tap.

  • WagepayDark hero card with a single big white number ($687.40). Subtitle in faded white. The only screen element with a coloured background — earns its weight by being the most important thing.
  • BetasharesStacked allocation bar inside the hero — graduated red→peach→amber→grey→black showing where the week's money goes. A pie chart hides the proportions; the bar makes them obvious.
  • BetasharesSection label as a quiet ALL-CAPS eyebrow ("TODAY'S BRIEFING") with a "all ›" link on the right. Doesn't compete with content.
  • TotoBriefing rows use the existing red brand for the bills card icon tint and reuse the family-context content (Mia, Riley, Bali countdown).
  • TotoFloating glass nav bar: lifts off the screen edge, semi-transparent with backdrop blur. Same pill shape as the hero CTA so the visual language repeats.

Screen 02

Wallet — money allocation

This is the page that most fintechs get wrong with a pie chart. Betashares uses a horizontal stacked bar and a list of "tickers" — both work because they reveal proportion at a glance and let you scan totals down a column.

  • BetasharesHorizontal stacked bar with rounded ends, segments in graduated reds + amber + grey + black. The whole budget at one read.
  • BetasharesTicker-style category codes (GROC, RENT, FUEL, DINE, BILLS) in bold ALL-CAPS, full name in muted grey below. Right-aligned big bold percentage with a small dollar figure underneath. Lifted directly from how Betashares lists holdings.
  • BetasharesColoured circle holders for the category icon. The colour matches the bar segment so the eye traces from bar to row.
  • WagepayCompact summary card at the top showing spent / left of budget — same role as Wagepay's "Available now" card.
  • TotoNo pies. No donuts. The bar + list does everything a donut chart would and more.

Screen 03

Plan — what's coming up

The Plan tab today is mostly a calendar grid. The Betashares "UPCOMING" pattern is better — a vertical list of cards where each card is a self-contained, scannable unit (icon, who-it's-for tag, name, location, when).

  • BetasharesUpcoming card row: pastel circle icon · ALL-CAPS tag (SWIM · MIA) · bold title · muted secondary line · right-aligned smart relative time ("In 2h", "Tomorrow", "16 days") with the actual time/date below.
  • BetasharesOutline-button setup steps ("Set frequency", "Set date", "Assign to") for adding new events — copies their AutoInvest setup pattern. Lower commitment than a full modal.
  • TotoPer-person tags (MIA, RILEY, FAMILY, VEHICLES) double as colour codes — each kid has a colour, household items have grey, vehicles have red.
  • TotoBottom red CTA ("+ Add event") because it's the page's primary verb. The little "Quick add" row sits above it for partial setups.

Screen 04

Home — household sections as a bento grid

Betashares uses a coloured-stripe bento card to brand each portfolio family (B.EARN, B.GREEN, B.TECH). The same pattern works for household sections — the colour stripe is the section's identity, the code is searchable, the glyph and one line of context tell you what's inside.

  • BetasharesThin coloured stripe along the top — strong enough to identify the section, quiet enough not to dominate the card. Each section gets its own colour.
  • Betashares"B." prefix codes (B.HOME, B.AUTO, B.KIDS, B.PETS, B.HEALTH, B.DOCS) — gives the household a coherent brand and reads fast. Could expand to B.MEALS, B.NOTICES later.
  • BetasharesSoft-tinted glyph holder — pastel circle behind each icon that picks up the stripe colour at low saturation.
  • TotoOne concrete hook per card ("$4,200 outstanding", "Rego due 14 May", "Vet visit in 3 days") so the grid isn't just navigation — it's a status check.

Screen 05

Kids — points dial & chore list

Wagepay's "Bank Score" / "Credit Score" dial is the perfect visual for kids' weekly chore points. It turns an abstract number into something a 7-year-old can read in two seconds: how full is the ring?

  • WagepayBig circular dial — grey track, red fill, big bold number in the centre with "of 300" muted below. Resets every Sunday.
  • WagepayPill-shaped CTA ("Open chore list") in primary colour — same shape as Wagepay's "Withdraw Cash".
  • BetasharesProfile pill row at the top (🦊 Riley · 🐰 Mia) — single tap switches kids. Active pill goes black; inactive pills stay light. Same pattern Betashares uses for portfolio toggles.
  • TotoDone chores fade to grey with strikethrough title and faded points. Pending chore stands up — bright check border, full red points.
