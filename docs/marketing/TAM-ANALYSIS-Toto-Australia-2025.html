Methodology

How This Was Built

All population data sourced from the ABS 2021 Census (Australia's most recent census; next is 2026) and IBISWorld 2025 household projections. App user data from Sensor Tower, Similarweb, and public app store listings. Where exact figures are unavailable, conservative estimates are used and flagged.

Total Household Base IBISWorld projects 10.7M Australian households in 2024–25. ABS 2021 Census counted ~10.0M — IBISWorld's 1.8% annual growth rate applied gives the 2025 estimate.
ICP Sizing ABS 2021 Census family composition percentages applied to the 2025 household base. Cohorts are mutually exclusive by primary household type (no double-counting).
App Adoption Rate 97% smartphone penetration for Australian adults. Finance/household app adoption benchmarked at 30–40% of target cohort based on Sensor Tower AU personal finance data and global budgeting app adoption rates (~60% of smartphone users use at least one finance app).
Realistic User Target SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market) = households with smartphones in each cohort. SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market) = conservative 5–8% capture rate in year 3–5, based on comparable apps' growth trajectories.

Key Observations

What This Means for Toto

No single dominant competitor in AU

None of the four benchmarked competitors are Australian-built or AU-market-first. FamilyWall is European-led, GoodBudget and Skylight are US-led. Toto has first-mover advantage as an AU-native product.

No competitor combines finances + family OS

FamilyWall has the calendar, GoodBudget has the budget — but no single competitor does both well. This is Toto's clearest white space: the all-in-one household operating system with a genuine financial layer.

ICP 1 alone is a significant market

2.66M Australian households with children under 16 represents a ~$53M ARR opportunity at just $20/year per household — before touching ICPs 2, 3, or 4.

ICP 4 (Singles) is large but lower fit

3.08M single-person households is the largest cohort by raw numbers, but Toto's current feature set is optimised for multi-person households. Worth targeting later with focused positioning.

Skylight AU opportunity

Skylight is hardware-dependent (a physical wall calendar) — not widely available in Australian retail. This creates a direct opening for Toto to capture families who want Skylight-style organisation but in an app-only format.

Benchmark SOM is achievable

GoodBudget (~4M global users over ~12 years) averaged ~333K new users/year. Toto's AU SOM of 408K in 3–5 years is ambitious but comparable to what early-stage category leaders achieve in a single English-language market.

Expansion Priority Summary

🇦🇺 AU — Launch & prove the model

~408K SOM. Home market, ABS data is solid, no localisation needed. Capture ICP 1 families first. Target: 50K paying users by end of Year 1.

🇬🇧 UK — First international expansion

~396K SOM. Culturally closest to AU, English-language, similar household composition. FamilyWall has a foothold but no financial layer. Lower competitive intensity than US.

🇺🇸 US — Scale market

~1.15M SOM. 12× AU by household count. Requires bank CSV localisation, USD pricing, US-specific financial categories. Highest competition but highest ceiling — long-term prize.

Pricing leverage

The US market tolerates higher subscription prices than AU or UK. A $50/yr USD tier is viable there vs ~$29 AUD in AU. Localised pricing per market unlocks materially different ARR.

ICP 1 drives all three markets

Families with children under 16 represent the highest-fit, highest-capture cohort in all three markets. Product development should stay ICP-1-led even as new markets are entered.

Combined 3-market ceiling

At base pricing across all three markets at full SOM: ~$81M AUD ARR. This is a credible 5–7 year company-building target for a household OS with strong retention economics.
