Positioning

What Toto is and who it's for

"The app that runs your household."

Not a budget app. Not a meal planner. Not a to-do list. It's the one app that replaces the five apps, three spreadsheets, and constant mental load of running a home.

Target User

The household manager — the person who pays the bills, plans the meals, books the car service, remembers the rego, and knows where the insurance policy is. They're exhausted by the mental load and currently using a mess of disconnected tools.

Launch Strategy

Three phases from validation to growth

1
Validate
Weeks 1–4
  1. Landing page — single page at toto.app with the pitch, screenshots, and waitlist email capture. Use Carrd or simple HTML. One afternoon of work.
  2. Reddit seeding — post in r/AusFinance, r/AusProperty, r/MealPrepAustralia, r/YNAB. Genuine "I built this" posts. Australian finance Reddit is active and supportive of local tools. Don't sell — show.
    "I was sick of using 5 apps to run my household so I built one that does it all."
  3. Facebook Groups — Australian family/parenting groups: "Budgeting Mums Australia", "Meal Planning Australia", "First Home Buyers Australia". Same genuine approach.
  4. TestFlight / direct install — 50 families using it. Watch what they use daily vs what they ignore. The features that get daily opens are your product. Kill or hide the rest.
2
Content Engine
Weeks 4–12
  1. TikTok / Instagram Reels — short demos: "How I plan my family's entire month in 2 minutes." The meal planning to shopping list to budget flow is inherently visual and satisfying. One reel a week.
  2. "Monthly Money Reset" content series — a monthly ritual: review spending, plan meals, check maintenance, forecast costs. Position Toto as the tool for this ritual. Blog + socials.
  3. SEO play — target long-tail keywords Australians search:
    • "family budget app australia"
    • "meal planning shopping list app"
    • "when is my car rego due"
    • "household maintenance schedule template"
    One landing page per use case. All things Toto does.
  4. Podcast guest circuit — Australian personal finance podcasts (She's On The Money, The Money Cafe, Get Rich Slow Club). Story: "I built a family life assistant because no single app did everything."
3
Growth
Months 3–6
  1. Referral program — "Give a friend 1 month Pro free, get 1 month free." Families talk to families. School drop-off, sport sidelines, BBQs — word of mouth is the primary channel.
  2. Partnerships:
    • Meal kit companies (HelloFresh, Marley Spoon) — integrate recipes into the meal planner
    • Insurance comparison (Compare the Market, iSelect) — "Insurance expires in 30 days. Compare renewals?"
    • Car servicing (Ultra Tune, mycar) — "Service due. Book here?"
    These become revenue streams beyond subscriptions.
  3. Financial advisor channel — advisors need tools to recommend to clients. Toto as a "homework tool" between meetings. Bulk licensing for advisory firms.

What Makes or Breaks It

Honest assessment of risks and advantages

What makes it

  • Meal planning gets people opening daily — that's the hook
  • The Today screen makes them feel in control — that's the retention
  • AI features feel magical enough to pay for — that's the conversion
  • Being Australian-specific is a moat — no US competitor can copy easily
  • Every feature reinforces the others — meal plans link to shopping lists link to budget. The more you use, the harder it is to leave.

What breaks it

  • Onboarding takes too long — if it takes more than 3 minutes to feel useful, people drop
  • AI feels gimmicky — must save real time, not just demo well
  • Trying to be everything — better to nail the core loop than ship 20 mediocre features
  • Building in isolation — if you spend 6 months building and 0 months talking to users
  • Technical debt — single-file architecture needs rebuilding for scale, multi-user, and App Store
